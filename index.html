About Me

Dr. Amgad Farah graduated from the Faculty of Medicine, Khartoum College of Medical Sciences and Technology, Khartoum, Sudan. She did her Masters degree in Public Health from the University of Medical Sciences and Technology. Her study and involvement with a number of projects at the university helped develop her passion for reproductive and child health. The projects and initiatives extended via the university focused on using innovative qualitative research, community readiness assessment, critical incident techniques, verbal autopsy, participatory ethnography evaluation research, policy research and so much more to help generate a better understanding of communities with poor reproductive and child health systems.

Among the projects she was involved were: 1. Community Partnership Project in Kassala State, East Sudan 2. Mama is a researcher, evaluator and change agent (MamaREC) in Kassala State, East Sudan 3. Innovative Participatory Health Education Approach – Promote Health to Hard to Reach Conflict Affected Women 4. Rebuilding Reproductive and Child Health Systems in Post Conflict Settings’ Initiative (ReReCHI) 5. Qualitative Assessment of Impact of In-hospital Delay on Emergency Obstetric Care Services in Post- Conflict Setting

She has also developed an interest in using data analytics and is currently in the process of completing a certificate in data analytics from George Washington Professional College, United States. Dr. Amgad Farah continues to be determined to follow her passion in reproductive and child health and build her capacity to help others by participating in many future projects by pursing a masters degree in public health - maternal and child health at George Washington University, United States.
